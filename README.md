# Cross-Generator AI Review Detection

This repository studies a practical question: **does a detector trained on one
text generator recognize reviews produced by another?**

We compare genuine English product reviews with reviews generated by four model
families spanning GPT-2-era generation, LFM2.5-1.2B, DeepSeek-v4-pro, and
GLM-5.2. The primary detector is a rank-64 Rs-QLoRA adapter over a quantized
Qwen2.5-0.5B model.

## Main finding

The completed experiments reveal a strong but bounded transferable signal.
Detectors transfer well among the three tested modern generators, while transfer
between those generators and GPT-2-era reviews is almost absent.

Rs-QLoRA detection recall (rows train the detector; columns test it):

| Train \ test | GPT-2 | LFM | DeepSeek | GLM |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **GPT-2** | 98.1% | 0.3% | 0.5% | 0.1% |
| **LFM** | 0.1% | 99.8% | 53.4% | 44.5% |
| **DeepSeek** | 0.3% | 85.5% | 99.8% | 97.2% |
| **GLM** | 0.2% | 91.7% | 98.2% | 99.3% |

Each cell averages three training seeds: 1998, 7, and 42.

- Mean diagonal recall: **99.3%**.
- Mean off-diagonal recall among LFM, DeepSeek, and GLM: **78.4%**.
- DeepSeek and GLM transfer almost symmetrically: **97.2–98.2%**.
- LFM shares part of the modern signal, but its learned boundary is narrower.
- GPT-2 and the modern generators form nearly disjoint detection domains.

This supports a **transferable signal among the tested modern review datasets**.
It does not establish a universal detector for arbitrary AI-generated text.

## Unseen-generator evaluation

The stricter leave-one-generator-out (LOGO) experiment trains on a balanced pool
of the other three generators. Human reviews appearing in the held-out test set
are explicitly excluded from training.

| Unseen generator | Rs-QLoRA recall | Human FPR | TF-IDF recall | Human FPR |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| GPT-2 | 0.1% | 0.48% | 0.8% | 1.65% |
| LFM | 91.1% | 1.92% | 84.2% | 6.40% |
| DeepSeek | 95.1% | 2.13% | 92.3% | 5.60% |
| GLM | 96.7% | 2.01% | 90.4% | 6.02% |

Pooled Rs-QLoRA therefore detects an unseen modern generator with **91–97%
recall at approximately 2% human false-positive rate**. Neither Rs-QLoRA nor
TF-IDF transfers successfully to held-out GPT-2.

## Supporting findings

### Matched-generator accuracy

| Generator-specific detector | Accuracy, mean ± sample SD |
|---|---:|
| GPT-2 | 98.497% ± 0.154% |
| LFM | 99.776% ± 0.097% |
| DeepSeek | 99.694% ± 0.057% |
| GLM | 99.252% ± 0.071% |

### Negative control

A human-only dataset of 20,214 genuine reviews was assigned balanced random
pseudo-labels. The same training pipeline produced 49.31% accuracy and 48.95%
AUROC, as expected at chance. This argues against label leakage capable of
creating the headline result without a genuine class signal.

### Efficiency and baselines

On the DeepSeek dataset, Rs-QLoRA and standard QLoRA reach essentially the same
accuracy ceiling. Rs-QLoRA reduces mean H100 training time from 32.0 to 17.3
minutes—a **45.9% reduction or 1.85× speedup**—and peaks near 2.05 GiB of
PyTorch-allocated VRAM.

| Matched-distribution model | Test accuracy | AUROC |
|---|---:|---:|
| RoBERTa-base | 99.435% | 99.982% |
| DeBERTa-v3-base | 99.806% | 99.996% |
| ModernBERT-large | 99.700% | 99.996% |
| Rs-QLoRA, three-seed mean | 99.617% | 99.960% |

DeBERTa is the strongest matched-distribution baseline. The Rs-QLoRA result is
primarily about efficient adaptation and cross-generator behavior, not a new
peak in matched-test accuracy.

## Reproduce the study

### CPU: TF-IDF transfer and LOGO baselines

```bash
git clone https://github.com/PurplexyFlower/Fake-review.git
cd Fake-review
uv venv
source .venv/bin/activate
uv pip install datasets scikit-learn scipy
python runs/cross_gen_matrix.py --no-neural
```

### GPU: complete modern study

Use a Linux CUDA machine:

```bash
bash cloud/setup.sh
bash cloud/run_all.sh
```

The default `modern` scope runs:

1. dataset preparation;
2. three Rs-QLoRA and three standard QLoRA DeepSeek runs;
3. twelve generator-specific cross-generator runs;
4. four LOGO runs;
5. the human-only negative control;
6. TF-IDF, head-to-head, and transformer baselines;
7. the structured transfer matrix.

The queue is resumable. A completed job is skipped only when its configuration,
dataset hash, metrics, predictions, and adapter are all present. Required-phase
failures make `run_all.sh` exit nonzero instead of silently producing a partial
study.

`SCOPE=all bash cloud/run_all.sh` additionally runs the legacy
reviewer-rebuttal suite. It requires many more GPU-hours and is not needed to
reproduce the modern headline results.

## Datasets

Each generator dataset is balanced between genuine (`OR`) and generated (`CG`)
reviews. Generated reviews are grounded on genuine products and target the same
category, rating, and approximate length.

| File | Generated-review source | External copy |
|---|---|---|
| `dataset/modern_reviews_gpt2.csv` | GPT-2-era Kaggle data | repository |
| `dataset/modern_reviews_lfm.csv` | LFM2.5-1.2B | [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/Flowerly/modern-fake-reviews-lfm) |
| `dataset/modern_reviews_deepseek.csv` | DeepSeek-v4-pro | [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/Flowerly/modern-fake-reviews) |
| `dataset/modern_reviews_glm.csv` | GLM-5.2 | [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/Flowerly/modern-fake-reviews-glm) |

The `pooled_wo_*.csv` files are the leakage-controlled LOGO training datasets.
The human-only permutation-control CSV is deterministic and regenerated by the
pipeline rather than committed.

Generation prompts, sampling parameters, raw/final SHA-256 values, provider
records, and explicitly unrecorded details are documented in
[DATA_PROVENANCE.md](DATA_PROVENANCE.md). The committed raw generation CSVs are
the canonical artifacts because hosted API aliases are mutable and decoding was
not seeded.

## Claim boundaries

- The experiments cover English product reviews, not arbitrary domains or
  languages.
- Generator identity is confounded with prompting, sampling, and dataset
  provenance. The shared modern signal may include generation-pipeline effects.
- The modern generators use a common grounded-review prompt template.
- Neural LOGO currently uses one training seed per held-out generator.
- Cross-generator datasets can share grounding products even when their review
  texts differ.
- Robustness to paraphrasing, adversarial rewriting, new model families, and
  future generators has not been established.

The defensible conclusion is therefore narrow but useful: **within these
datasets, modern generators share a detectable and transferable review-writing
signal, while GPT-2-era generation occupies a separate detection domain.**
